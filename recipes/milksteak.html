Milk Steak

Description

"I'm gonna want the milk steak, boiled over hard, and a side of your finest jelly beans, raw." - Charles Kelly

A staple dish of many high-end restaurants in Paris and Nice, the milk steak is fine dining experience like no other. A dish enjoyed by business men from all ventures; bridge builders, to denim traders, to oil men and even full-on-rapists, you can now enjoy at home, with this simple recipe from Charles Kelly.

Ingredients

  • 22oz Porterhouse Steak or Filet Mignon
  • 4 pints of full fat cow's milk
  • Raw jellybeans

Steps

  1. Bring milk to a vigourous boil in a large pot
  2. Drop in steak and lower heat to a slow boil
  3. Bubble away until the steak is boiled hard, and is an exquisite pale grey
  4. Once hard, remove from the steak from the pot and serve immediately while steaming
  5. Top with fresh, uncooked jellybeans and enjoy
